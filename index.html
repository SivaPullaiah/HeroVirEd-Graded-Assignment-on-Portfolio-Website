Blockchain technology has emerged as a groundbreaking innovation, disrupting traditional industries and revolutionizing the way we conduct transactions and share data. With its decentralized and transparent nature, blockchain is poised to transform various sectors. My GATE experience

Title: Demystifying the GATE Exam Pattern: A Pathway to Success in Engineering Admissions

The GATE (Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering) exam follows a well-defined pattern designed to assess a candidate's comprehensive understanding of engineering concepts. By understanding the exam pattern, aspiring candidates can formulate effective strategies for preparation and optimize their performance on exam day. The GATE exam consists of a total of 65 questions carrying 100 marks. These questions are divided into two types: Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs) and Numerical Answer Type Questions (NATs). MCQs, the most common question type, have four options, of which only one is correct. They carry one or two marks each. On the other hand, NATs require candidates to enter the numerical answer using a virtual keypad. These questions carry one or two marks each as well. Negative marking is applicable for MCQs. For each incorrect response in MCQs carrying one mark, 1/3 mark is deducted, while for each incorrect response in MCQs carrying two marks, 2/3 marks are deducted. However, there is no negative marking for NATs. By understanding the question distribution, types, and negative marking scheme of the GATE exam, candidates can adopt a targeted and balanced approach to their preparation, focusing on areas that require more attention and practicing time management skills to complete the exam within the allocated time frame.
